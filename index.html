Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet is over five years old, damaged, doesn't fit properly, or lacks modern safety features, it's time for a new one. Regularly check for cracks, strap wear, and interior damage. Safety standards evolve, so consider upgrading for better protection.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

After storage, check tires, brakes, fluids, battery, and lights. Start the bike, listen for issues, and take a short test ride. If unsure, consult a mechanic for inspection before riding

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a helmet, obey traffic laws, signal, and stay alert for safety.
